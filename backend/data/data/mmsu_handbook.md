# Mariano Marcos State University (MMSU) Student Handbook
*Academic Policies, Rules, and Guidelines Replica*

---

## Article I: Classification and Admission of Students

### Section 1: Student Classification
1.  **Regular Student:** A student who is officially enrolled in a curriculum and carries the full semestral load prescribed in that curriculum.
2.  **Irregular Student:** A student who does not carry the full semestral load or who is out of sequence in terms of prerequisite subjects due to failures, dropping, or shifting.
3.  **Shifter:** A student who has been allowed to change their program of study within the same college or to another college within MMSU.
4.  **Transfer Student:** A student who enters MMSU after having been enrolled in another higher education institution.

### Section 2: Maximum Residency Rule (MRR)
*   The maximum residency allowed for students to complete their program is **1.5 times the normal duration of the curriculum**.
*   For a 4-year degree program (such as BSCS), the MRR is **6 academic years** (excluding official leaves of absence).
*   For a 5-year degree program, the MRR is **7.5 academic years**.
*   Students who exceed the MRR must appeal to the College Dean and the Vice President for Academic Affairs for a residency extension.

## Article II: Academic Load and Prerequisites

### Section 1: Semestral Academic Load
*   The normal academic load of a student is the total number of units prescribed in the curriculum for that specific semester.
*   **Overloading:** A student is not allowed to enroll in more than the prescribed units, except in the following cases:
    1.  **Graduating Students:** During their final two semesters, graduating students may be allowed to overload by a maximum of 6 units, subject to the approval of the Department Chair and Dean.
    2.  **Honor Students:** Students with a Weighted Average of 1.75 or higher in the preceding semester may overload by up to 3 units, provided they do not have any failing grades.

### Section 2: Prerequisite Subject Rules
*   No student shall be permitted to enroll in a subject if they have not passed its prerequisite subject(s).
*   **Co-requisites:** A subject designated as a co-requisite must be taken either prior to or concurrent with the main subject. If the co-requisite is failed, it must be retaken.
*   **Waiver of Prerequisites:** Prerequisites may be waived *only* in exceptional cases for graduating students, provided they have previously sat in the prerequisite class but failed it, and both subjects are offered in the same semester. The waiver must be approved by the Department Chair, College Dean, and University Registrar.

## Article III: Grading System and Scholastic Standing

### Section 1: The Grading Scale
MMSU utilizes the following numerical grading system:

| Grade | Description | Equivalency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1.00** | Excellent | 98 - 100% |
| **1.25** | Highly Meritorious | 95 - 97% |
| **1.50** | Very Good | 92 - 94% |
| **1.75** | Meritorious | 89 - 91% |
| **2.00** | Good | 86 - 88% |
| **2.25** | Very Satisfactory | 83 - 85% |
| **2.50** | Satisfactory | 80 - 82% |
| **2.75** | Fairly Satisfactory | 77 - 79% |
| **3.00** | Passing | 75 - 76% |
| **4.00** | Conditional | Retake or complete removal exam |
| **5.00** | Failure | Retake required |
| **INC** | Incomplete | Incomplete course requirements (must complete within 1 year) |
| **DRP** | Dropped | Officially dropped from the class before midterm |

### Section 2: Scholastic Delinquency
1.  **Warning:** A student who fails, drops, or receives an INC in 25% to 49% of their total enrolled units in a semester shall receive an official warning from the Department Chair.
2.  **Probation:** A student who fails or drops 50% to 75% of their enrolled units shall be placed on probation for the subsequent semester. Their academic load will be restricted to a maximum of 15 units.
3.  **Dismissal:** A student who fails more than 75% of their academic load in a semester, or who fails to clear their probationary status, shall be dismissed from the College.

## Article IV: Guidelines for Shifting and Transferring

### Section 1: Internal Shifting Requirements (Within College)
To shift to another program within the same College (e.g., within CCIS, from BSIT to BSCS):
1.  The student must submit a written request endorsed by their Academic Advisor and the Department Chair.
2.  The student must possess a cumulative General Weighted Average (GWA) of **2.25 or higher**.
3.  There must be an available slot in the receiving department.

### Section 2: External Shifting Requirements (Between Colleges)
To shift to a program in another College (e.g., from COE to CCIS):
1.  The student must have completed at least two semesters of residency in their current college.
2.  The student must possess a GWA of **2.00 or higher** with no failing grades (5.00) or unresolved INC grades on their record.
3.  The student must pass the interview and qualifying exam of the receiving college.
4.  The shift must be approved by the Deans of both colleges and the University Registrar.

## Article V: Graduation and Academic Honors

### Section 1: Graduation Requirements
*   A student shall be recommended for graduation only after satisfying all curricular requirements, including completing all required academic units, physical education (PE), and NSTP courses.
*   **Thesis/Capstone Clearance:** For BSCS students, the final thesis must be successfully defended, revised, approved by the panel, and hard-bound copies deposited in the College Library.
*   **Clearance Form:** A completed multi-department clearance form must be signed by all relevant offices (Library, Accounting, Cashier, Registrar) prior to graduation.

### Section 2: Graduation with Honors (Latin Honors)
Students who complete their programs with high scholastic achievements may graduate with honors under the following GWA brackets:

*   **Summa Cum Laude:** GWA of **1.00 to 1.20**
*   **Magna Cum Laude:** GWA of **1.21 to 1.45**
*   **Cum Laude:** GWA of **1.46 to 1.75**

**General Disqualifiers for Latin Honors:**
1.  Having a grade lower than **2.50** in any academic or non-academic course (including PE and NSTP).
2.  Having a failing grade (5.00) or an un-removed INC grade on their transcript.
3.  Having exceeded the normal duration of the curriculum (e.g., graduating in 5 years instead of 4 for BSCS).
4.  Having been subjected to disciplinary action for academic dishonesty (e.g., cheating, plagiarism).

## Article VI: Student Conduct and Academic Integrity

### Section 1: Academic Dishonesty
*   **Cheating:** Any form of cheating during exams, quizzes, or assignments is strictly prohibited. First offenses will result in a zero grade for the assessment. Subsequent offenses will be escalated to the Student Disciplinary Tribunal, resulting in suspension or expulsion.
*   **Plagiarism:** Submitting work that is not original (including copying source code or capstones from other student repositories or using un-credited online sources) constitutes plagiarism. Plagiarized theses or capstones will result in an automatic grade of 5.00 and suspension.

### Section 2: Grading Appeals
*   A student who believes they received an erroneous grade has the right to appeal.
*   The appeal must be filed in writing to the Instructor within **15 days** of the official release of grades.
*   If unresolved, the appeal is escalated to the Department Chair and College Dean. Grade adjustments require submitting an official Grade Change Form signed by the Instructor, Chair, Dean, and University Registrar.

## Article Vll: Leaves of Absence, Honorable Dismissal, and Re-admission

### Section 1: Leave of Absence (LOA)
*   A student who wishes to temporarily stop studying must apply for an official Leave of Absence (LOA).
*   The LOA application must state the reason (e.g., financial, health, personal) and be approved by the College Dean.
*   An official LOA excludes that semester/year from the Maximum Residency Rule (MRR) calculation.
*   Unofficial leaves (stopping without filing LOA) will be counted against the MRR, and the student will be categorized as "AWOL" (Absence Without Official Leave).

### Section 2: Honorable Dismissal and Transferring out
*   A student in good standing who wishes to transfer to another institution must apply for Honorable Dismissal.
*   Once granted, the student is cleared of all academic and financial obligations to MMSU.
*   An Honorable Dismissal is irreversible; the student cannot be re-admitted to MMSU without undergoing the full transfer entrance protocols.

## Article Vlll: Student Organizations and Clubs

### Section 1: Recognition of Orgs
*   All student organizations, clubs, and fraternities/sororities must be officially recognized by the Office of Student Affairs (OSA) at the beginning of each academic year.
*   Unrecognized groups are prohibited from booking campus facilities, posting announcements, or holding assemblies.
*   Fraternities or sororities engaged in hazing, violence, or unauthorized initiations will have their recognition permanently revoked, and involved students will face expulsion.

### Section 2: Financial Accounts
*   All student organization funds must be deposited in an official bank account or managed via the College Cashier.
*   Treasurer reports and audits must be submitted to the Student Council and OSA at the end of each semester before clearance is signed.

## Article IX: Library Policies and Clearances

### Section 1: Borrowing Privileges
*   Regular students with valid MMSU ID cards may borrow up to **5 books** at a time for a duration of **7 days**.
*   Reserve books and reference materials can only be read within the library premises or borrowed overnight (after 4:00 PM to be returned by 9:00 AM the next day).
*   **Overdue Fines:** Overdue books incur a fine of **₱5.00 per day** per book (excluding Sundays and holidays).

### Section 2: Library Clearance
*   Students must obtain library clearance at the end of every semester.
*   All borrowed books must be returned and all outstanding fines paid before the Library Director signs the clearance form. Unresolved library holds will prevent students from enrolling in the next semester.

## Article X: Physical Education and NSTP Requirements

### Section 1: Physical Education (PE) Courses
*   All undergraduate students must complete **4 PE courses** (total of 8 units) as part of their graduation requirements:
    *   PE 1: Physical Fitness and Gymnastics
    *   PE 2: Rhythmic Activities
    *   PE 3: Individual and Dual Sports
    *   PE 4: Team Sports and Games
*   Students with physical disabilities must submit a medical certificate to the PE Department to be assigned modified, low-intensity academic alternatives.

### Section 2: National Service Training Program (NSTP)
*   All Filipino undergraduate students must complete **2 semesters of NSTP** (NSTP 1 and NSTP 2) in one of the following components:
    *   ROTC (Reserve Officers Training Corps)
    *   CWTS (Civic Welfare Training Service)
    *   LTS (Literacy Training Service)
*   NSTP components cannot be shifted halfway; both semesters must be completed in the same program branch.
